Dr.A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

"If you want to shine like a sun, first burn like a sun."

Biography

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, often referred to as the "Missile Man of India," was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He emerged as one of India's most prominent scientists and served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Kalam's journey from humble beginnings to becoming a renowned aerospace engineer and a visionary leader is truly inspirational. He graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and went on to study Aerospace Engineering at Madras Institute of Technology. His significant contributions to India's missile and nuclear programs earned him recognition and respect both nationally and internationally. He played a crucial role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and the successful test-firing of the indigenous ballistic missile, Agni, making India self-reliant in missile technology. His leadership as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and later as the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) paved the way for technological advancements in defense and space sectors. Apart from his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam was a passionate advocate for education and youth empowerment. He believed in harnessing the potential of India's youth for the nation's progress. He often interacted with students and inspired them with his vision of a developed India, urging them to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals. After his presidency, Kalam continued to be an influential figure, engaging in various educational and societal initiatives. He authored several books, including his autobiography "Wings of Fire," which remains an enduring source of inspiration for millions around the world. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of scientific brilliance, leadership, and patriotism. He is remembered as a true patriot, a people's president, and a source of inspiration for generations to come.
